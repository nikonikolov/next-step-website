Exams

These most often include the following:

  • SAT Reasoning Test

    • The hardest one of these is the SAT Reasoning Test. You should as always start preparing early and do check what exam dates are available at your nearest test center. Note that in long term, the best preparation for this test is READING CLASSICAL AMERICAN LITERATURE from 18th, 19th and 20th century. This will include at least 2 or 3 books. Remember that a really good SAT result can bring you extra points. In short term, you can best prepare by solving a lot of sample tests in EXAM CONDITIONS. We DO recommend buying the official SAT exam book from CollegeBoard. An outstanding result will generally be anything above 2300. Usually if you score more than 2150 you should not worry, but remember test scores alone do not mean much if they are not reinforced by a strong application.

  • SAT Subject Tests

    • These should not be hard for a bright mind like you and you should definitely try to score the maximum. Barrons' exam books for the specific test should be a good preparation resource. Note there is usually some material that is taught in 11th and 12th grades in Bulgarian Schools. This should not stop you from taking the test earlier than that and still scoring the maximum. Again, DO a lot of sample tests!

  • TOEFL/IELTS

    • After taking the SAT Reasoning Test, this should feel like a piece of cake. You do not necessarily need a really high result on this test - universities require it as an evidence you can speak English. A score higher than 105 would usually be all you need.


Although the above may sound easy, you should definitely take your time to study properly. You can often find yourself spending weeks preparing only for a single exam. Note as well the reference scores provided above are not at all conclusive and might vary accross universities. For details, check the Tests/Exams section on the target university's website. Do not forget you should usually register for a test approximately a month before it takes place

Extra Flair

Probably the MOST IMPORTANT piece of your application. Remember that most of the other applicant will also have excellent test results and school records. This section can help you shine by showing your accomplishments in your field of study. These will usually include International Competitions and Olympiads, some of which are listed in the Competitions section on our website. Note that a single medal at International Olympiad might not mean a lot (we might be wrong here: we are speaking from personal experience but there have been occassional cases that have proven this statement wrong). Generally, you will be expected to show a good track of accomplishments at International Level and it will be marvellous if you can show accomplishements in more than one field - this will really make you stick out. This is hardly achieved overnight and requires a lot of persistence and studying. However, it remains one of the most important parts of your application. On a separate note: to be allowed to partricipate in International Olympiads you are expected to be in top few at the respective National Olympiad. For more information and possibly preparation ask your school teacher. You should be also able to find the information you need on Google. Now go to the Subjects section on our website and start studying.
